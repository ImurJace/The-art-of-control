Cognitive Preferences

Each type is made up of four cognitive preferences:

  • Introversion (I) / Extraversion (E) – How you focus your energy.
  • Sensing (S) / Intuition (N) – How you process information.
  • Thinking (T) / Feeling (F) – How you make decisions.
  • Judging (J) / Perceiving (P) – How you organize your life.

Dark Psychology & Influence

Dark psychology is the study of manipulation, coercion, and psychological tactics used to influence or control others. It explores the darker aspects of human nature and how they can be used for both good and bad intentions.

1. Psychological Manipulation

  • Gaslighting – Making someone doubt their reality.

    • Gaslighting: Gaslighting is a form of psychological manipulation where an individual makes another person doubt their perception of reality, memories, or sanity. It is commonly used in abusive relationships, workplaces, and even politics to gain control over others.

    Signs of Gaslighting:

    • Denial of Reality – The manipulator denies things that happened, even with evidence.
    • Twisting the Truth – They distort facts to confuse the victim.
    • Blame-Shifting – Making the victim feel responsible for the manipulator’s actions.
    • Minimizing Feelings – Discrediting emotions to make the victim feel irrational.
    • Rewriting History – Changing past events to align with their narrative.

    Effects of Gaslighting:

    • Increased self-doubt and confusion.
    • Emotional dependency on the manipulator.
    • Anxiety, depression, and lowered self-esteem.
    • Difficulty trusting one’s own perception and decisions.

    How to Counter Gaslighting:

    • Keep a Record – Document conversations and events.
    • Trust Your Feelings – Your emotions and experiences are valid.
    • Seek Support – Talk to trusted friends or professionals.
    • Set Boundaries – Limit interactions with manipulative individuals.
    • Guilt-Tripping – Using guilt to control behavior.
    • Love Bombing – Overwhelming affection to gain trust.
    • Silent Treatment – Withholding communication as punishment.

    2. Persuasion & Influence Tactics

    • Reciprocity Principle – People feel obligated to return favors.
    • Authority Bias – People trust figures of authority.
    • Social Proof – People follow the behavior of the majority.
    • Scarcity Effect – The rarer something is, the more valuable it appears.

    3. Dark Triad Personalities

    • Machiavellianism – Deceptive, strategic, and manipulative behavior.
    • Narcissism – Extreme self-interest and lack of empathy.
    • Psychopathy – Lack of guilt, impulsiveness, and charm.

    4. Covert Psychological Tactics

    • Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) – Using language to subtly influence thoughts.
    • Framing – Presenting information in a way that alters perception.
    • Mirroring – Copying behavior to build rapport.
    • Anchoring – Using a reference point to steer decisions.

    5. Ethical Use of Influence

    Understanding dark psychology can help in self-defense against manipulation and ethical persuasion in business, leadership, and communication.
